Steering Wheel Issues

Symptoms

  • Steering wheel shakes or vibrates, especially at high speed
  • Steering feels too hard or too loose
  • Car pulls to one side while driving
  • Clicking, whining, or grinding noise when turning
  • Steering wheel does not return to center after a turn

Faulty Parts (Causes)

  • Wheel alignment out
  • Unbalanced wheels
  • Worn suspension parts (tie roads, ball joints)
  • Low or leaking power steering fluid
  • Damaged power steering pump
  • Worn steering rack
  • Uneven or damaged tyres

Easy hacks or temporary tricks

  • Check and top up power steering fluid
  • Reduce speed if steering shakes at high speed
  • Check tyre pressure and inflate properly
  • Avoid sharp turns if steering feels stiff

Note: These are temporary only, not permanent fixes.

Time Required to fix

  • Wheel alignment or balancing: 30 to 60 minutes
  • Power steering fluid top-up: 10 minutes
  • Suspension or rack repair: 2-4 hours

Cost level

  • Tyre pressure or fluid top-up: low
  • Alignment or balancing: Medium
  • Steering rack or pump replacement: High

Proper Solution

  • Get wheel alignment or balancing done
  • Replace worn suspension components
  • Fix power steering fluid leaks
  • Replace steering rack or pump if damaged
  • Replace damaged or uneven tyres

When to visit a Mechanic

  • Steering wheel shakes badly even at low spees
  • Steering becomes very hard or suddenly loose
  • Fluid leaking under the car
  • Steering makes loud grinding noise
  • Car becomes difficult to control
