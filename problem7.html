High RPMS on Idle

Symptoms

  • Engine RPM stays very high while car is not moving
  • RPM goes up and down on its on
  • Engine sounds loud or aggressive at idle
  • Car jerks when shifting gears
  • Fuel consumption increases

Faulty Parts (Causes)

  • Dirty or faulty Idle Air Control (IAS) valve
  • Vaccum leak (cracked hose)
  • Dirty throttle body
  • Faulty Throttle Position Sensor (TPS)
  • Engine coolant temperature sensor problem
  • Stuck throttle cable
  • AC load or sensor malfuction
  • ECU issue (rare)

Easy hacks/ Temporary tricks

  • Turn AC off and check RPM
  • Gently tap throttle pedal to see if RPM settles
  • Check for loose or broken vvaccum hoses
  • Restart engine and let it idle for a few minutes

Time Required to Fix

  • Throttle body/ IAS cleaning: 30-45 minutes
  • Vaccum hose replacement: 20-30 minutes
  • Sensor replacement: 30-60 minutes

Cost level

  • Throttle body cleaning: Low
  • Vaccum hose or IAS: Medium
  • ECU or wiring issue: High

Proper Solution

  • Clean or replace Idle Air Control valve
  • Clean throttle body
  • Fix vaccum leaks
  • Replace faulty TPS or temperature sensor
  • Check and adjust throttle cable
  • Reset ECU idle learning (if needed)

When to Visit a Mechanic

  • RPM stays above normal continuously
  • Engine stalls after revving
  • Check engine lights turns ON
  • RPM fluctuates badly
  • Car becomes hard to control in traffic

Normal Idle RPM

Petrol cars: 700-900 RPM

Diesel Cars: 600-800 RPM

If idle RPM is much higher, something is wrong.
