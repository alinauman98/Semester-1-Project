Car Leaks

Symptoms

  • Puddles under the car after parking
  • Low fluid warnings on dashboard
  • Burning smell or sweet smell
  • Engine overheating or steering getting hard
  • Brake pedal feels soft
  • Unusual smoke from engine bay

Faulty Parts (Causes)

Oil leak

  • Worn engine oil gasket or seal
  • Loose or damaged oil filter
  • Cracked oil pan

Coolant Leak

  • Leaking radiator
  • Cracked coolant hose
  • Faulty radiator cap
  • Bad water pump

Transmission Fluid Leak

  • Worn transmission seal
  • Loose transmission pan

Brake Fluuid Leak

  • Damaged brake line
  • Faulty master cylinder

Power Steering Fluid Leak

  • Leaking steering hose
  • Bad steering rack seal

Easy hacks/ Temporary tricks

  • Identify fluid by color and smell
  • Top up fluid to reach a safe place
  • Tighten loose hose clamps(if visible)
  • Avoid long drives
  • Park on cardboard to track leak source
  • Note: Do not ignore brake or coolant leaks

Time Required to Fix

  • Hose or clamp fix: 20-40 minutes
  • Gasket or seal replacement: 1-3 hours
  • Major leak (radiator/rack): 3-6 hours

Cost level

  • Hose clamp or top-up: Low
  • Seal or hose replacement: Medium
  • Radiator/ steering rack/ transmission repair: High

Proper Solution

  • Replace damaged hoses
  • Replace worn gaskets or seals
  • Repair or replace radiator
  • Fix brake lines immediately
  • Repair power steering system
  • Clean area and recheck for leaks

When to Visit a Mechanic

  • Brake fluid leak (urgent)
  • Coolant leaking + overheating
  • Fluid level drops quickly
  • Burning smell or smoke
  • Steering becomes hard
  • Leak increases over time
