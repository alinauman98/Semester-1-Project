Car Overheating

Symptoms

  • Temperature gauge goes above normal or into red
  • Steam coming from the bonnet
  • Coolant warning lights turns on
  • Burning smell
  • Engine loses power or suddenly shuts off
  • Coolant leaking under the car

Faulty Parts (Causes)

  • Low coolant level
  • Radiator leak or blockage
  • Faulty radiator fan
  • Water pump failure
  • Stuck thermostat
  • Blown head gasket
  • Old or dirty coolant
  • Broken radiator cap

Easy hacks/ Temporary Tricks

  • Note: Only for emergency situations
  • Turn AC OFF, turn heater ON full
  • Stop the car and let engine cool down
  • Check and add coolant or water (only when engine is cool)
  • Drive slowly at low RPM
  • Open bonnet to release heat (engine OFF)

Never open radiator cap when engine is hot

Time Required to Fix

  • Coolant top-up: 10-15 minutes
  • Fan or thermostat fix: 1-2 hours
  • Radiator or water pump repair: 2-4 hours
  • Head gasket repair: 1-2 days

Cost level

  • Coolant refill/ cap: Low
  • Fan or thermostat: Medium
  • Radiator or pump: High
  • Head gasket: Very High

Proper Solution

  • Fix coolant leaks
  • Replac faulty radiator fan
  • Replace thermostat
  • Flush and refill coolant system
  • Repair or replace radiator
  • Head gasket replacement if damaged

When to Visit a Mechanic

  • Temperature rises within minutes
  • Steam keeps coming out
  • Overheating happens again and again
  • Coolant disappears quickly
  • Engine oil looks milky (head gasket issue)
  • Engine shuts off due to heat

Note: Driving an overheated car can destroy the engine. If temperature goes into red, stop immediately.
